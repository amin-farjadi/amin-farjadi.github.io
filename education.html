MSc in Advanced Control & Systems Engineering (With Industrial Placement)

Sept '18 – Sept '20 The University of Sheffield

Thesis title: “Integrity Attacks in Smart Factories”

Supervisor: Dr Iñaki Esnaola

I secured an opportunity to research the cybersecurity aspect of the 5G-Encode Projcet—part of UK Government’s "Industrial 5G Testbeds and Trials: Manufacturing and Logistics” programme. In collaboration with National Composites Centre (NCC), false data injection attacks are constructed and analysed for the manufacturing processes at NCC. Detection methods based on Bayesian and frequentist inference will be analysed and compared. The process will be simulated, and the effect of attacks measured.

BEng (Hons) in Mechanical Engineering

Spet '15 – June '18 University of Bristol

Research title: Lie Detector

Supervisor: Dr Andrew Lawrie

Algorithm and numerical method for solving non-linear differential equations was developed. The Lie Detector algorithm was inspired by Lie Algebra and set a methodical approach for extracting the underlying differential equation of big data. This was done using computational linear algebra and supervised learning techniques such as principal component analysis (PCA) and coordinate transformation. The research received 2 awards from the Institution of Mechanical Engineering and obtained the highest score (77%) for independent research in the cohort. The framework has been used for particle image velocimetry (PIV) at the fluid dynamics lab of Department of Mechanical Engineering, University of Bristol.

Graduated with First Class Honours.

Accredited by: IMechE.
